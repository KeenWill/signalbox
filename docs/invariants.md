# Invariant catalog

This catalog states constraints the future design must make enforceable. “Accepted” records current direction. “Provisional” means the constraint or its exact boundary needs an ADR. Each invariant has a primary classification; actual enforcement may use several layers.

## Domain and identity

| ID | Invariant | Class | Status | Expected enforcement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| INV-001 | Owner-global durable-command identifiers and identifiers for sessions, accepted inputs, turns, turn attempts, model calls, tool requests, tool attempts, runners, and semantic transcript entries are distinct and not interchangeable. A claimed durable-command identifier names one discriminated caller payload and its terminal applied-or-rejected result across all command kinds, sessions, and clients for that owner. | Domain/type-level | Accepted distinction; names, command scope, and accepted-input boundary proposed in ADR-0001 | Separate opaque domain types; boundary conversion validates kind, namespace, ownership, and scope. |
| INV-002 | Domain types are separate from transport messages, storage records, and framework types. | Domain/type-level | Accepted | Explicit mapping modules and dependency checks; no generated or ORM type in domain transitions. |
| INV-003 | Session creation cause and transcript ancestry are independent facts. | Domain/type-level | Accepted | Separate fields/types; no single mutually exclusive enum combining both concepts. |
| INV-004 | Logical work and every physical attempt have separate identities and lifecycle records; every turn has one typed durable origin, while accepted steering remains separately identified. A tool request belongs to one turn, and each tool attempt belongs to one request and one issuing turn attempt. Identity is selected by typed transitions, never by semantic comparison of free-form intent. | Domain/type-level | Logical/physical split accepted; origin and tool-ownership precision proposed in ADR-0001 | State-machine types prevent a physical retry from overwriting logical identity or steering from masquerading as a new turn. |
| INV-005 | Semantic conversation content, operational coordination, audit/recovery evidence, transient streaming, and client presentation are distinct representations even when correlated. | Domain/type-level | Accepted | Purpose-specific domain projections; no universal event type serving all roles. |
| INV-006 | Each transition is valid only from explicitly permitted prior states; terminal turns and physical outcomes do not return to running. No turn terminalizes until every owned logical tool/approval dependency is durably closed or terminally non-dispatchable, every issued physical operation is classified, its current attempt is ended, and any durable wait is closed. `Completed`, `Refused`, `Failed`, and `Cancelled` additionally require every physical ambiguity to have resolving evidence or a durable owner-accepted-risk marker; unresolved unacknowledged ambiguity may terminalize only as `ReconciliationRequired` with its exact ambiguity marker. | Domain/type-level | Accepted in principle; exact turn/attempt state sets and terminal guard proposed in ADR-0004 | Total transition functions over typed states plus persistence guards. |

## Durability and concurrency

| ID | Invariant | Class | Status | Expected enforcement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| INV-007 | Once the hub acknowledges accepted input, its identity, content, session, explicit delivery request, ordering, and enough disposition/configuration information to recover its promised treatment are durable. | Transaction-level | Accepted durability; exact no-active-turn and active-work lifecycle proposed in ADR-0027 | Persist the accepted input and initial disposition in one committed transaction before acknowledgement. |
| INV-008 | Accepted logical work has durable immutable effective configuration and explicit provenance sufficient to explain later semantic choices. Session defaults are versioned and affect only subsequently accepted origin input. Explicitly configured origins record request, defaults version, and effective value; pending steering stores one binding to its source turn, from which a reclassified origin derives that turn's immutable effective value. The closed value explicitly represents model/alias meaning, instructions, disabled tools or a normalized nonempty enabled-tool set with unconstrained/canonical nonempty placement constraints, disabled retry/fallback, resource choices, and interpreting policy versions; operational facts remain excluded. Disabled tools cannot carry placement, and empty/no-op tool forms are not distinct values. | Transaction-level | Accepted provenance; exact algebra, freeze, defaults, binding, and equality rules proposed in ADR-0027 | Create logical work and complete provenance atomically; normalize before comparison, compare constructible semantic values, and never duplicate source identity/configuration or reread current defaults or alias definitions as historical intent. |
| INV-009 | At most one logical turn actively progresses in a session; every proposed `Active` phase retains the slot. `Active(Running)` owns exactly one current attempt whose `Prepared`, `Running`, or `StopRequested` variant is the single nonterminal state. `StopRequested` is cancellation-only or fatal mismatch with a nonempty failure set and optional accepted cancellation; it preserves additions in either event order and through restart and prohibits new semantic effects. Matching terminal variants make fatal-stop completion/refusal/cancellation, stop-after-yield, and no-stop cancellation unrepresentable. A prepared attempt ends with the turn on cancellation. Waiting phases carry their exact wait subject and no attempt. Every operation issued by an attempt is terminally classified before the attempt yields to a wait. Every accepted-input-origin turn retains immutable order facts outside lifecycle state. `Queued` has no start payload; eligibility preserves order while atomically constructing nonoptional starting lineage/frontier carried by every active or terminal state. First-in-session is valid exactly when no prior turn exists, and `After` names the terminal turn immediately before it. Future non-input origins must define their own order/start payload and lineage. | Database-level | Single-turn rule accepted; exact active-state, attempt membership, stop causes, queue order, and accepted-input starting-lineage construction proposed in ADR-0004 and ADR-0027 | Database uniqueness/exclusion or serialized aggregate transition, not process memory alone. |
| INV-010 | Queued work, confirmation waits, and delegated-result waits remain durably represented across hub restart. | Database-level | Accepted | Nonterminal states and referenced inputs persist without dependence on a live connection. |
| INV-011 | A stale physical attempt or dispatch generation cannot advance or overwrite current logical state. Tool-result application validates the attempt's owning request, issuing turn attempt, and current dispatch generation. | Transaction-level | Accepted; tool ownership cardinalities proposed in ADR-0001 | Compare-and-set against current ownership, attempt, and generation in the same result-acceptance transaction. |
| INV-012 | Duplicate command or result delivery cannot create duplicate logical work or apply one physical outcome twice. Owner-global command identity lookup across all command kinds, sessions, and clients precedes current-state validation. The first committed handling of an unseen well-formed typed command under established owner authority atomically records its discriminated payload and terminal applied-or-rejected result; the same payload returns that result, including a rejection after state changes, while reuse for another variant, session, or payload is rejected. Malformed transport, pre-authority, and pre-commit infrastructure failures that do not reach committed domain handling do not claim the identifier. | Database-level | Accepted in principle; owner-global command scope, precedence, and mismatch behavior proposed in ADR-0001 and ADR-0027 | One owner-wide transactional uniqueness boundary plus command application/rejection; result-delivery deduplication details remain operation-specific. |
| INV-013 | Archiving changes discoverability/lifecycle state but does not erase durable history; restoration preserves identity. Exact archive and restore transitions remain open. | Database-level | Provisional pending the archive lifecycle decision | Preserve identity and history while ADR-0028 defines permitted transitions; destructive retention remains a separate future policy. |

## Model execution

| ID | Invariant | Class | Status | Expected enforcement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| INV-014 | Every hub authorization to attempt a provider interaction has a model-call identity and records the requested model selection, frozen alias meaning when applicable, exact hub-resolved provider/model target, and context frontier, even if later send preparation fails. Target resolution precedes call creation; resolution failure creates no targetless call. Provider-reported identity and mismatch are appended as typed evidence when available. The first trusted mismatch observed while the outcome-eligible call is nonterminal immediately selects known failure and requests best-effort cancellation. After terminal ambiguity it preserves physical disposition, failing the turn when no other unacknowledged ambiguity remains and requiring reconciliation otherwise. After current-authority completion but before turn terminality it preserves the call/history, appends one invalidation bound to the exact call and first evidence, validates target and authority from canonical call/transfer records, stops new effects, and fails or reconciles the turn after classification. Ordinary refusal without fatal stop terminalizes call/attempt/turn atomically, so mismatch delivered with it is classified first; a continuation refusal raced under fatal stop remains non-authoritative. After terminal known failure/cancellation it preserves that state and existing precedence. Post-transfer evidence is non-authoritative, while post-turn-terminal evidence cannot rewrite committed content or context. | Transaction-level | Requested/resolved/reported provenance accepted; creation, retry, and mismatch disposition proposed in ADR-0001 and ADR-0005 | Freeze alias meaning at turn acceptance; resolve and persist the target and call before sending; classify observable target evidence without reopening terminal state. |
| INV-015 | Every model call records the exact ordered semantic context frontier it consumed; a turn is not assumed to have one immutable frontier. | Transaction-level | Accepted | Freeze and persist each call frontier before issuing the provider request. |
| INV-016 | Safe-point steering never mutates an already-issued provider request, tool request, approval, or tool attempt, and accepted steering never disappears without a durable disposition. The baseline safe point requires every earlier issued physical operation for the turn to be classified and every earlier logical tool/approval dependency to have a durable outcome; an authorized-but-undispatched tool request remains blocking. Steering captures inherited configuration provenance; every later prepared call, including a duplicate-risk replacement, consumes eligible pending steering atomically. The consumed disposition references that immutable call rather than duplicating its turn/frontier, and committed steering remains in every later retry or continuation frontier. | Runtime validation | No-mutation/no-loss direction accepted; provider-call-only safe point, physical/logical dependency guards, provenance, and reclassification proposed in ADR-0027 | Consume steering atomically at the allowed boundary, preserve it in later frontiers, or reclassify it visibly with source-turn provenance. |
| INV-017 | Any hub-controlled fallback is explicit, policy-authorized, and visible in call provenance. | Operational policy | Accepted; whether fallback exists in version one is open | No adapter-local silent fallback; persist reason and source/target resolution. |
| INV-018 | A safety refusal does not by itself authorize automatic fallback or model substitution. In the proposed baseline and without fatal stop, refusal from the outcome-authoritative provider call atomically commits explicit refusal content and terminalizes call, attempt, and turn; serial orchestration makes unrelated owned work alongside that ordinary transition invalid. A continuation that races refusal under `StopRequested(FatalMismatch)` remains physically refused but its content is non-authoritative and the turn fails or reconciles. Mismatch delivered with or before ordinary refusal makes refusal material non-authoritative and the call fails; mismatch resolving terminal ambiguity preserves that physical state, producing failure only when no other unacknowledged ambiguity remains and reconciliation otherwise; evidence learned after an atomically refused turn cannot rewrite it. Closing a duplicate-risk provider wait atomically consumes eligible steering, creates the replacement attempt and call, and transfers outcome authority, so later prior-call evidence cannot create a competing conversational outcome. | Operational policy | No-fallback constraint accepted; baseline turn, mismatch, and replacement-authority dispositions proposed in ADR-0005 | Classify target evidence and refusal separately, serialize replacement against resolving evidence, persist every call and authority transfer, and reject implicit fallback, retry, or prior-call outcome competition. |

## Input, ancestry, and clients

| ID | Invariant | Class | Status | Expected enforcement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| INV-028 | Input declares an explicit treatment valid for authoritative session state: start when no turn is active or, during active work, interrupt, next safe point, or after current turn; the chosen treatment is durable. | Protocol compatibility | Three active-work intents accepted; no-active command and validation proposed in ADR-0027; default UX provisional | Make the resulting command field explicit before persistence and reject stale state races before acknowledgement. |
| INV-029 | Interrupt stops future progress as promptly as safely possible and never claims to undo an issued effect. It atomically ends an unsent prepared attempt, constructs `StopRequested(CancellationOnly)` for an already-running attempt while retaining the slot through classification, populates cancellation on an existing fatal-mismatch stop without reauthorizing work, or closes an exact durable wait. An unacknowledged ambiguous issued effect remains physically ambiguous while the interrupted turn terminalizes as reconciliation required; an already owner-accepted ambiguity retains both its physical outcome and risk marker while later cancellation is classified normally. The interrupt-created turn is the immediate successor. | Operational policy | No-rollback direction accepted; exact three-way transition and successor behavior proposed in ADR-0004 and ADR-0027 | State-specific cancellation transitions plus honest outcome classification; successor activation waits for predecessor terminal disposition where necessary. |
| INV-030 | A fork identifies an immutable source session and transcript frontier; later source changes do not rewrite the fork. | Database-level | Accepted for single-source ancestry | Persist source and frontier identity with the new session. |
| INV-031 | A delegated child has a distinct session identity, is independently browsable, and has an explicit relationship to the parent work. This invariant does not determine whether related sessions archive independently, cascade, or block archival. | Domain/type-level | Accepted; archival coupling pending ADR-0028, with result and cancellation semantics also provisional | Separate session identity plus typed relationship, never embedded as transient substate only; ADR-0028 must define archival behavior. |
| INV-032 | A reconnecting client can reconstruct authoritative durable state and replace any transient draft without treating lost deltas as final content. | Protocol compatibility | Accepted | Snapshot/version protocol plus clearly marked transient updates. |

## Compatibility and operations

| ID | Invariant | Class | Status | Expected enforcement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| INV-033 | Unknown or incompatible protocol versions and required fields fail explicitly rather than being silently reinterpreted. | Protocol compatibility | Provisional pending protocol ADR | Negotiation rules and compatibility fixtures. |
| INV-034 | A startup scan derives the complete evidence and stop-cause set before ending every prior-process nonterminal turn attempt with disposition `Lost` in its matching no-stop, cancellation, or fatal terminal variant. `Lost` is reserved for the abandoned orchestration tenure; stop causes already durable or first established by the same scan remain explicit. The scan separately classifies each interrupted physical operation as completed, known failed, refused where applicable, cancelled, or ambiguous; it is not an attempt and issues no semantic effects. Live and startup classification use the same precedence: unacknowledged ambiguity first (wait, or reconciliation with cancellation/fatal stop), then sufficient outcome-authoritative non-mismatched completion/refusal only without fatal stop, known failure or current-authority mismatch invalidation, and confirmed cancellation. Mismatch evidence never reopens a terminal call: after ambiguity it supports turn failure when no other ambiguity remains and reconciliation otherwise; after completion during an active turn it appends invalidation and stops future effects; ordinary refusal without fatal stop terminalizes the turn atomically, while a refusal raced under a prior or same-scan fatal stop remains physical evidence and failure/reconciliation controls; after known failure/cancellation it preserves state and precedence; after authority transfer or turn terminality it cannot change semantic outcome. Evidence may otherwise refine the ambiguity set, while owner-authorized continuation records accepted risk only when no fatal invalidation prohibits it. | Runtime validation | Accepted classification direction; exact scan, attempt, ambiguity, and mismatch dispositions proposed in ADR-0004 and ADR-0005 | Idempotent startup scan and explicit durable transitions; no cancellation-only or classification-only replacement attempt and no reopening terminal operations. |
| INV-035 | Provider credentials remain under hub control in the initial architecture and are not sent to clients or general-purpose runners. | Operational policy | Accepted initial direction | Secret access boundary and adapter interfaces; sanitized logs and protocol schemas. |

## Review rule

A feature proposal must cite the invariants it relies on and add a scenario when it introduces a new lifecycle edge. If an implementation cannot enforce an accepted invariant, the change must revise the design through an ADR rather than weakening the invariant implicitly.
